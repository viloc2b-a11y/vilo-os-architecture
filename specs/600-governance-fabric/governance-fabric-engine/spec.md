\# Governance Fabric Engine



Status: Draft



\---



\# Objective



Define the governance architecture responsible for audit lineage, protocol compliance, monitoring intelligence, deviation intelligence, CAPA orchestration, inspection readiness, and operational explainability.



\---



\# Core Principle



Governance is not a disconnected QA layer.



Governance emerges continuously from runtime execution and immutable lineage.



\---



\# Responsibilities



The Governance Fabric Engine must support:



\- audit lineage

\- protocol compliance computation

\- monitoring intelligence

\- deviation intelligence

\- CAPA orchestration

\- inspection readiness

\- operational explainability

\- workflow integrity

\- role attribution

\- document traceability



\---



\# Core Governance Entities



\- Deviation

\- CAPA

\- Monitoring Finding

\- Query

\- Audit Artifact

\- Inspection Evidence

\- Compliance Signal

\- Workflow Exception

\- Signature Event

\- Protocol Variance



\---



\# Governance Runtime Model



Governance state derives from:



\- immutable operational events

\- runtime lineage

\- protocol graph

\- workflow execution

\- source integrity

\- role attribution

\- unresolved operational state



\---



\# Deviation Intelligence



The engine must support:



\- automatic deviation detection

\- missed procedure detection

\- window violation detection

\- unresolved workflow detection

\- protocol variance tracking

\- repeated issue detection

\- root cause support



\---



\# CAPA Orchestration



The engine must support:



\- CAPA creation

\- CAPA linkage

\- corrective action tracking

\- preventive action tracking

\- unresolved CAPA monitoring

\- workflow escalation

\- accountability attribution



\---



\# Monitoring Intelligence



The engine must support:



\- monitoring findings

\- unresolved findings

\- repeated findings

\- risk scoring

\- workflow-linked findings

\- source-linked findings

\- protocol-linked findings



\---



\# Inspection Readiness



The engine must continuously generate:



\- reconstructable lineage

\- attributable actions

\- protocol traceability

\- source traceability

\- workflow explainability

\- signature traceability

\- audit-ready timelines



Inspection readiness should exist continuously, not only before audits.



\---



\# Explainability



Every operational state must be explainable from:



\- events

\- lineage

\- protocol graph

\- workflow history

\- actor attribution



No “magic state” should exist.



\---



\# Operational Signals



The engine must compute:



\- coordinator overload risk

\- unresolved workflow risk

\- deviation probability

\- protocol complexity risk

\- monitoring risk

\- safety escalation risk



\---



\# Operational Event Integration



Governance signals derive from immutable events such as:



\- visit\_locked

\- source\_signed

\- procedure\_missed

\- deviation\_created

\- workflow\_overdue

\- query\_opened

\- safety\_escalated



\---



\# UX Requirements



The coordinator and QA teams must see:



\- unresolved risks

\- unresolved findings

\- pending CAPAs

\- missing signatures

\- protocol deviations

\- inspection-sensitive gaps



Without manual spreadsheet reconstruction.



\---



\# MVP Scope



MVP must support:



\- immutable lineage

\- protocol traceability

\- deviation tracking

\- CAPA workflows

\- monitoring findings

\- audit reconstruction

\- inspection-ready timelines



\---



\# Deferred Scope



Deferred:



\- enterprise quality management system replacement

\- sponsor-wide RBQM platform

\- external auditor portals

\- advanced predictive governance AI



\---



\# Explicit Non-Goals



The Governance Fabric Engine is NOT:



\- static QA documentation

\- spreadsheet-based oversight

\- disconnected monitoring tracker

\- generic ticketing software



\---



\# Final Rule



If operational truth cannot be fully explained during an inspection, the governance architecture has failed.
